About Me

Scientist at the frontier of nanomedicine & process engineering

I am a Postdoctoral Associate at MIT, working under the guidance of Professors Allan S. Myerson and Richard D. Braatz. My research focuses on the engineering of lipid nanoparticles (LNPs) for nucleic acid drug delivery — including mRNA therapeutics and gene therapy — with an emphasis on precision manufacturing and morphological control.

I completed my PhD in Chemical Engineering at KU Leuven (Belgium), where I studied nucleation kinetics, crystallization processes, and the role of ultrasound and microfluidics in pharmaceutical manufacturing. I was supported by a prestigious Research Foundation Flanders (FWO) fellowship and spent eight months as a Visiting Scientist at Johnson & Johnson.

Beyond the bench, I am passionate about science communication, entrepreneurship, and mentoring the next generation of engineers. I am enrolled in MIT's Research to Entrepreneurship program, Conception X, and have completed the MIT Kaufman Teaching Certificate and Georgia Tech's Leadership & Management in Action Program.

Research Focus

Engineering matter at the nanoscale

My work spans three interconnected domains: the design and manufacturing of lipid nanoparticles, the fundamental science of crystallization, and the development of advanced microfluidic systems for continuous pharmaceutical processing.

01 ──
💊

Lipid Nanoparticle Engineering

Designing mRNA-loaded lipid nanoparticles with precise size and morphology control for nucleic acid drug delivery. Developing mechanistic models via population balance equations to understand LNP formation and optimize therapeutic efficacy.

02 ──
🔬

Crystallization Science

Investigating primary and secondary nucleation kinetics, the role of fluid shear, ultrasound, and acoustic streaming in crystal formation. Applying these insights to continuous pharmaceutical crystallization processes.

03 ──
⚗️

Microfluidics & Process Intensification

Designing and characterizing modular microfluidic systems — impinging jet mixers, tubular microcrystallizers — for scalable, continuous manufacturing of active pharmaceutical ingredients and nanoparticles.

04 ──
📡

Hyperspectral Imaging & AI

Applying data-driven modeling strategies and hyperspectral imaging techniques to lyophilization processes, enabling real-time monitoring and advanced quality control in pharmaceutical manufacturing.

05 ──
🌊

Fluid Mechanics & Mixing

Studying advection–diffusion in polygonal microfluidic mixers and energy-dissipation rate analysis. Understanding the interplay between fluid dynamics and phase behavior in complex pharmaceutical systems.

06 ──
🧬

Nucleic Acid Therapeutics

Collaborating across disciplines to advance mRNA and gene therapy delivery platforms. Reviewing structure, morphology, and function of LNPs as vehicles for the next generation of biologics and vaccines.
